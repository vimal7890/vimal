Anglican Church of Canada

Intro

The Anglican Church of Canada is the Anglican Communion province serving Canada. It grew out of the Church of England's colonial presence, became self-governing in the nineteenth century, and now operates as a national church with its own primate, synods, and diocesan structure across the country.

Source: Intro adapted from the Wikipedia article "Anglican Church of Canada". Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Structure

The church follows the usual Anglican pattern of bishops, synods, and dioceses. Authority is shared among the national General Synod, four provincial synods, and the diocesan synods, while the primate serves as the senior national bishop.

Wikipedia summarizes the church as having 29 dioceses grouped into four ecclesiastical provinces. Those provinces are listed below in the same broad terms used in the church's overview material.

Ecclesiastical province Role General region
Canada Provincial synod grouping dioceses in Atlantic Canada and eastern Quebec Atlantic Canada and eastern Quebec
Ontario Provincial synod for most dioceses in Ontario Ontario
Rupert's Land Provincial synod spanning much of western and northern Canada Prairies, north, and parts of northwestern Ontario
British Columbia and Yukon Provincial synod for the Pacific region British Columbia and Yukon
