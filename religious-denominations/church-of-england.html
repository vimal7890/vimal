Church of England

Intro

The Church of England is the established Christian church in England and the Crown Dependencies and the historic mother church of the Anglican tradition. It traces its roots to early Christianity in Roman Britain and to Augustine of Canterbury's mission in 597. Its present form took shape during the English Reformation, when it broke from papal authority under Henry VIII and developed distinctive Anglican doctrine and worship. The British monarch is its supreme governor, while the Archbishop of Canterbury is its senior cleric. The church is organized through dioceses and parishes in England, the Crown Dependencies, and the Diocese in Europe.

Structure

The Church of England is organized into 42 dioceses. Thirty belong to the Province of Canterbury and 12 belong to the Province of York.

These dioceses cover England, the Isle of Man, the Channel Islands, a small part of Wales, and the Diocese in Europe. Each diocese has a cathedral, except Leeds, which has three co-equal cathedrals.
