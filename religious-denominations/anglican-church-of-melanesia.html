Anglican Church of Melanesia

Intro

The Anglican Church of Melanesia is the Anglican Communion province serving the Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, and New Caledonia. It grew out of the Melanesian Mission, became an autonomous province in 1975, and now organizes its congregations through dioceses led by the Archbishop of Melanesia.

Structure

The province follows the usual Anglican episcopal pattern, with dioceses, parishes, clergy, and a metropolitan archbishop. Wikipedia identifies the Diocese of Central Melanesia as the metropolitan see and describes the province as maintaining a diocesan system across its island territories.

The current provincial framework described on Wikipedia includes ten dioceses, listed below in a cleaned form.

Diocese Notes
Central Melanesia Metropolitan see of the Archbishop of Melanesia
Malaita One of the dioceses created when the province became autonomous in 1975
Vanuatu and New Caledonia Extends across the province's western island territories
Ysabel One of the original dioceses of the 1975 provincial structure
Temotu Added in 1981
Hanuato'o Added in 1991
Banks and Torres Added in 1996
Central Solomons Added in 1997
Guadalcanal Added in 2013
Southern Malaita and Sikaiana Newest diocese listed in the current Wikipedia structure summary
