Anglican Church of Southern Africa

Intro

The Anglican Church of Southern Africa is the Anglican Communion province serving South Africa and neighboring territories in the region. Formerly called the Church of the Province of Southern Africa, it traces its provincial identity to the nineteenth century and today is led by the Archbishop of Cape Town while covering dioceses in South Africa, Eswatini, Lesotho, Namibia, and Saint Helena.

Structure

The church uses standard Anglican episcopal polity, organizing parishes into dioceses, each led by its own bishop, while the provincial primate is the Archbishop of Cape Town. Wikipedia lists 25 dioceses in the present provincial structure.

Most dioceses are in South Africa, with one diocese each in Eswatini, Lesotho, Namibia, and Saint Helena. The province also notes that dioceses in Angola and Mozambique left in 2021 to form the separate Anglican Church of Mozambique and Angola.
