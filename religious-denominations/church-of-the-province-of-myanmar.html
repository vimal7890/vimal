Church of the Province of Myanmar

Intro

The Church of the Province of Myanmar is the Anglican Communion province for Myanmar. It became an independent province in 1970 after earlier links to the Church of England and the Church of India, Burma and Ceylon, and it now serves Anglican communities across the country through its own bishops and synodical structures.

Structure

Wikipedia describes the province as an episcopal church organized into dioceses, parishes, and provincial ministries. The bishop of Yangon serves as Archbishop of Myanmar, and the wider province is supported by diocesan bishops, assistant bishops, and church departments.

The diocesan structure summarized on Wikipedia includes the following six dioceses.

Diocese See Notes
Yangon Yangon Archbishop's diocese and provincial center
Hpa-an Hpa-an Diocese with St Peter's Cathedral
Mandalay Mandalay Diocese centered on Christ Church Cathedral
Myitkyina Myitkyina Developed from a missionary district in northern Myanmar
Sittwe Sittwe Diocese centered on St Mark's Cathedral
Toungoo Toungoo Missionary district later erected as a diocese in 1994
