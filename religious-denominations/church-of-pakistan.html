Church of Pakistan

Intro

The Church of Pakistan is a united Protestant church founded in 1970 that brings together Anglican, Presbyterian, Methodist, and Lutheran traditions within one national body. It belongs to the Anglican Communion while also participating in wider Protestant ecumenical organizations, and it is one of the largest Protestant churches in Pakistan.

Source: Intro adapted from the Wikipedia article "Church of Pakistan". Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Structure

The Church of Pakistan is governed nationally by a moderator and synod, but its local life is organized through dioceses led by bishops. Because it is a united church rather than a purely Anglican body, its governance blends episcopal oversight with wider Protestant synodical elements.

Wikipedia lists eight dioceses spread across the country. Together they cover the church's main regional centers, including Lahore, Karachi, Peshawar, and Sialkot.
