Church of South India

Intro

The Church of South India is a united Protestant church formed in 1947 by bringing together Anglican, Methodist, Presbyterian, Congregational, and Reformed traditions in South India. It is part of the Anglican Communion while also belonging to Methodist and Reformed world bodies, and it remains one of the largest churches rooted in the ecumenical movement in India.

Structure

The church is governed by a synod based in Chennai and headed by a moderator elected from among its bishops. Beneath the synod, local congregations are grouped into pastorates and then dioceses, combining episcopal leadership with strong diocesan and lay representation in administration.

Wikipedia states that the Church of South India is divided into 26 dioceses, including one in Jaffna, Sri Lanka. Its diocesan spread follows the church's historic footprint across the southern states of India and northern Sri Lanka.
