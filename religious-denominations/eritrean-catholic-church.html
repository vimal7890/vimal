Eritrean Catholic Church

Intro

The Eritrean Catholic Church is an autonomous Eastern Catholic church in Eritrea that uses the Alexandrian Rite in Ge'ez and was separated from the Ethiopian Catholic Church in 2015.

Source: Intro adapted from the Wikipedia article "Eritrean Catholic Church". Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Structure

There are four eparchies (bishoprics) in the country:

  • The metropolitan see is the Archeparchy of Asmara
  • The Eparchy of Barentu with faithful drawn largely from the Kunama people
  • The Eparchy of Keren with the highest percentage of the local population (nearly 12%) and with faithful drawn largely from the Bilen people
  • The Eparchy of Segheneyti in the country's Southern Administrative Region.
