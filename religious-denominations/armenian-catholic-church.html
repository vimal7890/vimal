Armenian Catholic Church

Intro

The Armenian Catholic Church is an Eastern Catholic church that follows the Armenian liturgical tradition, is in full communion with the bishop of Rome, and had roughly 757,726 adherents in 2017.

Structure

The Armenian Catholic Church is divided into Archdioceses, Eparchies, Apostolic Exarchates, Ordinariates for the Faithful of the Eastern Rite and Patriarchal Exarchates, each of which has functions similar to a diocese.

The Armenian Catholic Patriarchate of the See of Cilicia is the supreme authority of the Armenian Catholic Church. On 23 September 2021, Raphaël Bedros XXI Minassian was elected as the Church's new patriarch.

Religious orders

Armenian Sisters of the Immaculate Conception

The Order of the Armenian Catholic Sisters of the Immaculate Conception was founded in Istanbul on 5 June 1847 after being proposed in 1843 at the initiative of Archbishop Andon Hassounian. Around 1900 the sisters operated up to 30 schools across the Middle East, and in the United States they have run Armenian Sisters Academy schools in Philadelphia, Boston, and Los Angeles.

Patriarchal Congregation of Bzommar

The Patriarchal Congregation of Bzommar is an Armenian Catholic congregation of priests founded in 1750, whose members use the initials ICPB. It grew from a monastery established in Bzommar, Lebanon, by the Patriarch of Cilicia, and the congregation is dedicated to serving the Church and supporting Armenian communities wherever they are sent by the Catholicos, who serves ex officio as superior general.
