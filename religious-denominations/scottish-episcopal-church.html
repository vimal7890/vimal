Scottish Episcopal Church

Intro

The Scottish Episcopal Church is the Anglican Communion province in Scotland and presents itself as a continuation of the episcopal tradition that endured through Scotland's Reformation-era conflicts. It is a relatively small but historic church, led by a primus elected from among its bishops rather than by an archbishop with metropolitan jurisdiction.

Structure

The church is composed of seven dioceses, each with its own bishop, and it is governed nationally through a General Synod made up of bishops, clergy, and laity. The primus presides over the church but does not exercise the sort of metropolitan authority found in some other Anglican provinces.

Wikipedia's current list shows seven diocesan sees covering the whole of Scotland. The bishops are elected through diocesan procedures that involve both clergy and laity.
