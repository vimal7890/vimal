Church of the Province of West Africa

Intro

The Church of the Province of West Africa is an Anglican province spanning eight countries in West Africa and was formed in 1951 from a group of earlier dioceses that had grown out of missionary work in the region. It remains a multinational church, with Ghana as its largest national component, and is part of the wider Anglican Communion.

Source: Intro adapted from the Wikipedia article "Church of the Province of West Africa". Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Structure

The province is organized episcopally through dioceses, and Wikipedia's structure summary says those dioceses are grouped into two internal provinces: Ghana and West Africa. The primate serves the whole church, while each internal province has its own metropolitan archbishop.

This arrangement reflects the church's multinational footprint: Ghana contains the majority of the dioceses, while the second internal province links dioceses across the province's other West African member countries.
