Church in the Province of the West Indies

Intro

The Church in the Province of the West Indies is the Anglican province covering much of the Caribbean, with dioceses spread across island territories and mainland states in the region. It became a self-governing province in 1883 and remains a geographically scattered church whose ministry has long had to adapt to distance, travel costs, and dispersed communities.

Structure

The province is made up of eight dioceses across the Caribbean and nearby mainland territories. Its archbishop serves as primate of the province while also remaining a diocesan bishop, following the common Anglican pattern.

The dioceses are spread across island chains and mainland enclaves, so the structure balances regional identity with local diocesan oversight rooted in each territory's cathedral, clergy, and synod life.
