Church of the Province of South East Asia

Intro

The Church of the Province of South East Asia is an autonomous Anglican Communion province created in 1996 from the dioceses of Kuching, Sabah, Singapore, and West Malaysia. Based in Singapore, it serves Anglicans across much of Southeast Asia as well as Nepal, while its primate simultaneously remains a diocesan bishop within the province.

Structure

The province follows standard Anglican episcopal polity, with parishes organized into dioceses and the metropolitan archbishop chosen from among the diocesan bishops. Wikipedia's structure section notes that the province contains four dioceses, and that several of them are further subdivided into archdeaconries and deaneries.

Those four dioceses cover the church's historic centers in Singapore, Peninsular Malaysia, Sabah, and Sarawak-Brunei, while provincial oversight extends more broadly across the mission territories listed by the Anglican Communion.
