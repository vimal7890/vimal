Church of Ireland

Intro

The Church of Ireland is an autonomous Anglican church organized on an all-Ireland basis across the Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland. It is the island's second-largest Christian church after the Roman Catholic Church and preserves episcopal governance and parts of the older Irish Christian tradition while rejecting papal supremacy.

Structure

The Church of Ireland follows the standard Anglican pattern of parishes gathered into dioceses and dioceses gathered into provinces. Today it has 11 dioceses or united dioceses, split between the Province of Armagh and the Province of Dublin, while the General Synod serves as the church's central legislative body.

The Archbishop of Armagh is the church's senior primate and public leader, while the Archbishop of Dublin leads the southern province. Several dioceses are united, reflecting centuries of consolidation after the church's older medieval structure was reduced.
