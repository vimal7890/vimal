Anglican Church of Kenya

Intro

The Anglican Church of Kenya is the Anglican Communion province for Kenya and traces its roots to nineteenth-century missionary work in East Africa. It became a separate province in 1970 after Kenya and Tanzania were divided from the former Province of East Africa, and it now counts several million members across the country.

Structure

The church is governed episcopally through 41 dioceses, each headed by a bishop. The national primate is based at All Saints' Cathedral Diocese in Nairobi, while the dioceses are commonly grouped under four regional clusters that developed from older missionary centers.

Those clusters are Mombasa, Maseno, Mount Kenya, and Nakuru. Together they cover the whole country, from older coastal dioceses to newer missionary areas in northern Kenya.
