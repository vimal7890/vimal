Church in Wales

Intro

The Church in Wales is the Anglican church in Wales and became a separate, disestablished body in 1920 after being detached from the Church of England. It remains part of the Anglican Communion, keeps an episcopal structure, and combines both Welsh and English in its public life and worship.

Source: Intro adapted from the Wikipedia article "Church in Wales". Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Structure

The Church in Wales is made up of six dioceses, each with its own cathedral and bishop. The Archbishop of Wales is elected from among the diocesan bishops and does not hold a permanently separate archiepiscopal see.

Churchwide governance is exercised through the Governing Body, while diocesan and cathedral life remain the main local centers of administration, worship, and ordination.
