Episcopal Church

Intro

The Episcopal Church is the Anglican church based in the United States and several other jurisdictions, formed after the American Revolution when Anglicans in the new republic separated institutionally from the Church of England. It identifies itself as part of the worldwide Anglican Communion, preserves episcopal governance and apostolic succession, and has developed a broad internal range of evangelical, Anglo-Catholic, and broad-church traditions.

Structure

The Episcopal Church is a churchwide body with 106 dioceses grouped into nine provinces. Its presiding bishop serves as the church's primate, but the church is also governed synodically through General Convention, which brings bishops, clergy, and lay deputies together.

That governance pattern means authority is shared between national bodies and diocesan life. Provinces coordinate regional work, dioceses oversee congregations and clergy, and the House of Bishops and House of Deputies together make churchwide law and policy.
