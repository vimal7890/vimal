Church of the Province of Central Africa

Intro

The Church of the Province of Central Africa is an Anglican Communion province serving Botswana, Malawi, Zambia, and Zimbabwe. It became an independent province in 1955 and now brings together dioceses across those four countries under the Archbishop of Central Africa.

Source: Intro adapted from the Wikipedia article "Church of the Province of Central Africa". Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Structure

The province uses episcopal polity, with parishes grouped into dioceses and each diocese led by a bishop. Wikipedia's structure summary lists 15 dioceses spread across the four countries of the province, with the archbishop serving as primate and metropolitan.

The dioceses are unevenly distributed: one in Botswana, five in Zambia, five in Zimbabwe, and four in Malawi. The cleaned table below follows that country-by-country breakdown.
