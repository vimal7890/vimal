Episcopal/Anglican Province of Alexandria

Intro

The Episcopal/Anglican Province of Alexandria is an Anglican Communion province formed in 2020 from the old Diocese of Egypt with North Africa and the Horn of Africa. It covers Egypt, North Africa, and the Horn of Africa, and its primate also serves as Archbishop of Alexandria.

Source: Intro adapted from the Wikipedia article "Episcopal/Anglican Province of Alexandria". Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Structure

The province is organized into four dioceses created when the former Diocese of Egypt with North Africa and the Horn of Africa was elevated into a full province. The Archbishop of Alexandria is also the diocesan bishop of Egypt.

Together these dioceses cover eight countries listed in the Anglican Communion data for this site: Algeria, Djibouti, Egypt, Ethiopia, Eritrea, Libya, Somalia, and Tunisia.

Diocese Main see Territory
Diocese of Egypt Cairo and Alexandria Egypt
Diocese of North Africa Regional centers in North Africa Algeria, Libya, Tunisia
Diocese of the Horn of Africa Addis Ababa Djibouti, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Somalia
Diocese of Gambella Gambella Gambella region in Ethiopia
