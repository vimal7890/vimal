Church of North India

Intro

The Church of North India is a united Protestant church formed in 1970 by the union of Anglican, Methodist, Presbyterian, Congregational, and Disciples of Christ traditions working in northern India. It belongs to the Anglican Communion while also participating in Methodist and Reformed ecumenical bodies.

Structure

Wikipedia describes the church as combining episcopal, presbyterial, and congregational features. Its main governing body is the synod, which elects a moderator and deputy moderator, while diocesan bishops oversee the church's regional life.

The article also says the church spans northern and central India through a network of dioceses and pastorates. The core governance pattern can be summarized as follows.

Body or office Role
Synod Main administrative and legislative body of the church
Moderator Presiding bishop and chief public leader for a fixed term
Deputy Moderator Assists the moderator and shares synodical leadership responsibilities
Dioceses Regional bishoprics that organize parishes, institutions, and pastorates across the church's territory
Pastorates and parishes Local congregational units under diocesan oversight
