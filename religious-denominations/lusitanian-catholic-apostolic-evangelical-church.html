Lusitanian Catholic Apostolic Evangelical Church

Intro

The Lusitanian Catholic Apostolic Evangelical Church is an Anglican church in Portugal that emerged as a distinct Protestant body in the nineteenth century and later entered the Anglican Communion as an extraprovincial church. It preserves an episcopal structure and Portuguese-language worship while tracing part of its liturgical identity to both Anglican and older Iberian traditions.

Structure

The Lusitanian Church operates as a national extra-provincial Anglican church in Portugal rather than as part of a larger European Anglican province. It is led locally by its own bishop while remaining under the metropolitan authority of Canterbury within the Anglican Communion.

Its public life is organized around a national church structure, a cathedral seat at St. Paul's Cathedral in Lisbon, and Portuguese liturgical practice shaped by a locally developed prayer book that drew on Anglican, Roman, and Mozarabic forms.
