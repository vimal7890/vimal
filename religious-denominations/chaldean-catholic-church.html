Chaldean Catholic Church

Intro

The Chaldean Catholic Church is an Eastern Catholic church rooted in the Syriac tradition that uses the East Syriac Rite and has its historic center in Iraq.

Structure

The Chaldean Catholic Church is divided into a patriarchate, metropolitan archeparchies, archeparchies, eparchies, and territories dependent on the patriarch.

The Chaldean Catholic Patriarchate of Baghdad is the supreme authority of the Chaldean Catholic Church. On 12 April 2026, Paulos III Nona was elected as the Church's patriarch.

Religious orders

Daughters of Mary Immaculate

Daughters of Mary Immaculate is a Chaldean Catholic apostolic order with patriarchal rights, established in Baghdad on 7 August 1922 during the reign of Patriarch Mar Youssef Emmanuel II Thomas.

The Chaldean Sisters were founded by Father Anton Zebouni of Mosul after World War I to support the Chaldean community through apostolic service and prayer, with the congregation dedicated to Mary Immaculate.
